Rules and regulations

   

As a popular tourist destination, Singapore has certain rules and regulations that visitors are expected to follow. Here are some important ones to keep in mind:


Chewing gum is banned


It is illegal to chew or bring chewing gum into Singapore. If you are caught, you may be fined.


Littering is prohibited


Singapore is known for being a clean and green city, and littering is taken very seriously. Be sure to dispose of your trash properly.


Smoking is banned in public areas


Smoking is not allowed in public areas such as parks, beaches, and bus stops. Smoking is only permitted in designated smoking areas.


Drugs are strictly prohibited


The use, possession, or trafficking of drugs is illegal in Singapore, and the penalties can be severe, including imprisonment or the death penalty.


Dress appropriately


When visiting religious sites or places of worship, dress modestly and remove your shoes before entering. Swimwear should only be worn at the beach or pool.


Be respectful of local customs


Singapore is a multi-cultural society, and it is important to respect local customs and traditions. This includes avoiding loud or disruptive behavior in public places.


Always carry identification


Singapore requires visitors to carry identification at all times. A passport or government-issued identification card is acceptable.
